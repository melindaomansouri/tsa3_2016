Innovations and History

History

In September 17, 1782 the Montgolfier brothers in France, were the first to experiment with unmanned balloons. In preparation for their manned flights they sent unmanned aerostats aloft. In July 23, 1842, John Stringfellow and William Henson join together to make a steam engine to craft a propeller driven aerial vehicle called the Aerial Steam Carriage. This tests failed because it's engine was too heavy to support itself, however, this aided the transition from gliding planes to engined aircraft. On may 1, 1896 Samuel Langley successfully made a flying UAV in 1896 based off the steam engine. it was capable of flying for 3,300 feet with a maximum 100 ft altitude.

In January 1, 1944, the Germans developed an unmanned flying bomb called the Feisler FI 103. It is better known as V1 Flying Bomb, which was later seen as too dangerous and remade as a manned vehicle. Then in July of 1994 the MQ-1 predator was made. It was the first in the family of the MQ/RQ UAV’s. In most recent years UAV's are becoming available in public as toys, some with cameras, and some without. This means that there is massive room for improvement in the future!
